# Marketing Kit Rubric (Depth & Breadth)

This rubric is designed to ensure every section of the marketing kit achieves the depth, breadth, and richness of the gold-standard example. Use this to review, score, and iterate on each section.

---

## General Richness Criteria (apply to all sections)
- Multi-paragraph, layered insights (not just surface-level)
- Concrete examples, data, or scenarios relevant to the client/industry
- Actionable recommendations or next steps
- Mix of formats: narrative, bullets, tables, callouts
- Confident, professional, on-brand tone (no generic/vague language)
- Storytelling or narrative elements where appropriate
- Visual cues: suggests or includes tables, diagrams, or callouts
- Customization: tailored to the client’s context

---

## Section-by-Section Rubric

### 1. Overview
- Clearly states the purpose and intended use of the kit
- Explains the company’s unique position in the market
- Includes a bulleted or numbered list of what’s inside or how to use
- Sets expectations for the rest of the kit
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 2. The Goal
- Clearly articulates the primary business/marketing goal
- Describes target market/verticals and growth strategy
- Includes both narrative and actionable focus
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 3. Opportunity Areas
- Identifies at least 3–4 opportunity areas, each with a paragraph of explanation
- Uses data, trends, or examples to support each area
- Actionable recommendations for each opportunity
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 4. Key Findings
- Lists at least 5–6 key findings, each with a short narrative
- Includes data/statistics where possible
- Explains why each finding matters for the client
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 5. Market Landscape
- Describes macro trends and growth with data
- Includes competitor landscape and buying behavior
- Provides channel opportunities in a table or structured format
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 6. Audience & User Personas
- Defines at least 3–4 personas with profile, motivation, needs, messaging, demographics, psychographics, and buying behavior
- Uses narrative and bullet points for clarity
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 7. B2B Industry Targets
- Lists and describes key industry targets with NAICS codes, company types, and descriptions
- Includes tables or structured lists
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 8. Brand Archetypes
- Describes primary and secondary archetypes with mission, voice, values, and emotional promise
- Uses narrative and callout boxes/quotes
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 9. Brand Voice
- Defines brand essence, purpose, personality, tone, and voice examples
- Includes do’s and don’ts, taglines, and factual foundations
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 10. Content
- Outlines keyword opportunities, blog strategy, and structure
- Includes blog topic hubs, structure, and sample titles
- Meets all general richness criteria

### 11. Social Strategy
- Provides actionable framework for social content creation
- Specifies content mix, creative emphases, post types, and production checklist
- Includes campaign and landing page structure
- Meets all general richness criteria

---

## How to Use
- For each section, score each criterion on a 1–5 scale (1 = missing, 3 = meets, 5 = exceeds expectations)
- Flag “must have” vs. “nice to have” for your team
- Add reviewer notes and suggestions for improvement
- Use this rubric to guide iterative improvements until all sections meet or exceed the gold standard
